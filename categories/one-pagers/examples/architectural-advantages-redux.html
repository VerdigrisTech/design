Architectural advantages · Decision aid

Seven decisions that determine whether your power-intelligence layer compounds — or stalls.

For data-center operators evaluating the next decade of electrical observability and orchestration.

  1. Choose vendors with open APIs. Closed integrations create switching costs that exceed the software bill.
  2. Insist on raw waveform telemetry. 1 Hz averages discard the precursors that cause the failures you care about.
  3. Separate BMS from the diagnostic layer. A vendor doing both optimizes one at the expense of the other.
  4. Meter where load matters, not where access is convenient. Critical-circuit telemetry beats fleet averages.
  5. Cellular-redundant ingest. Network outages don't pause electrical events.
  6. Validate derate margins against measured load. Conservative derating strands 20-40% of existing capacity.
  7. Make diagnostics a contract, not a feature. Reliability obligations need their own SLA.
The argument

The next decade of reliability isn't about adding redundancy. It's about seeing the precursors today's monitoring stack flattens away — and orchestrating against them.
