Architectural advantages · Decision aid

Seven decisions that determine whether your power-intelligence layer compounds — or stalls.

For data-center operators evaluating the next decade of electrical observability and orchestration.

  1. Choose vendors with open APIs. Closed integrations create five-year switching costs that exceed the original software bill.
  2. Insist on raw waveform telemetry, not derived metrics. 1 Hz averages discard the precursors that cause the failure you care about.
  3. Separate the BMS from the diagnostic layer. A vendor doing both is optimizing one at the expense of the other.
  4. Deploy meters where load actually matters, not where access is convenient. Critical-circuit telemetry beats fleet-average telemetry on every reliability question.
  5. Standardize on cellular-redundant ingest. Network outages don't pause electrical events; the diagnostic layer must survive your IT incident.
  6. Validate your derate margins against measured load, not nameplate. Conservative derating strands 20-40% of your existing capacity; orchestration recovers it without new capital.
  7. Make the diagnostic layer a contract, not a feature. Reliability obligations need their own SLA, separate from the rest of the stack.
The argument

The next decade of data-center reliability isn't about adding redundancy. It's about seeing the precursors that today's monitoring stack flattens away — and then orchestrating against them.
