02Why we're here

Acme Life Sciences's R&D campuses run cleanrooms 24/7, but rectifier failures still surprise the team.

From the November 2025 site assessment with your facilities and reliability leads.

Three findings from the assessment carried into this pilot:

  • Rectifier failures arrive without warning. Three unplanned outages in the last 12 months, two of them weekend-only events that took 6+ hours to recover.
  • BMS dashboards don't see electrical-waveform structure. Power factor and harmonic distortion show up only after a fault, never as a leading indicator.
  • Energy spend is an opaque budget line. ~$2.4M/year across the four campuses; no visibility into which circuits drive the bill.

04Expansion criteria

Three measurable outcomes that earn the path to fleet deployment.

Same wording as the contract. If we hit all three, we move to fleet rollout.

  • Rectifier-health visibility. Signals identifies the precursor to at least one rectifier-class fault during the 90-day window, with at least 14 days of advance warning.
  • Baseline electrical waste. Quantify the 4-campus waste floor across power factor, harmonic distortion, and circuit imbalance, expressed in $/year against the current $2.4M baseline.
  • Operator adoption. At least 6 of 8 named operators access Signals weekly across the second half of the pilot (post-day-45), unprompted.
