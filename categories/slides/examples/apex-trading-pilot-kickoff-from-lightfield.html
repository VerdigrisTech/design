02Why we're here

Trading-engine racks run at 90%+ of nameplate, but power-quality margin is currently invisible.

From the April 2026 introductory call with the Apex Data Center Strategy team.

Three findings carried forward from the call:

  • Density is the constraint, not floor space. The trading-engine rows are pushed close to PDU thermal envelope to keep latency-critical workloads on copper distance; any unexpected derate event takes revenue-bearing capacity offline.
  • BMS dashboards stop at the PDU. Per-rack waveform structure, transient events, and harmonic distortion are not visible to the existing monitoring stack. Faults surface as alarms, never as leading indicators.
  • The infra team owns uptime but doesn't own the diagnostic data. When a rack misbehaves, root-cause investigation depends on vendor logs that arrive days later, in formats that don't cross-correlate with what the trading floor saw.

04Expansion criteria

Three measurable outcomes that earn the path to multi-hall rollout.

Same wording as the contract. If we hit all three, we move to fleet rollout.

  • Power-quality margin visibility. Signals identifies the precursor to at least one PDU-class derate event during the 90-day window, with at least 7 days of advance warning.
  • Density-headroom quantification. Quantify the safe per-rack density headroom across the three halls, expressed as %-of-nameplate the racks can sustain without entering the harmonic regime that triggered the original concern.
  • Operator adoption. At least 4 of 6 named operators access Signals weekly across the second half of the pilot (post-day-45), unprompted, and reference its data in at least one root-cause investigation.
