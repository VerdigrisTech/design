02The wedge

BMS sees that a rectifier failed. We see it failing.

8 kHz waveform telemetry on every monitored circuit, 24/7, for the life of the asset.

Most data center reliability tools sample at 1 Hz or below. Faults precipitate in milliseconds. The math doesn't add up; the precursor signal is there, but the resolution to see it isn't.

Verdigris meters sample at 8 kHz — fine enough to read harmonic structure, capacitor aging, and circuit-imbalance trends weeks before they become outages. We've validated this across 800+ rectifiers in production at four named customers.

05Why now

AI workloads are breaking the assumptions power systems were sized against.

  • Load swings are faster and larger. A GPU training run can pull 600 kW one second and 120 kW the next; rectifiers sized against steady-state 1990s assumptions degrade in ways their nameplates can't predict.
  • Capacity is the constraint, not capital. Hyperscaler-class operators routinely strand 20-40% of nameplate capacity to derating margins, because the alternative — operating closer to the limit — requires visibility nobody has.
  • The reliability gap is asymmetric. A 30-second outage in an AI training cluster costs more than a 30-day window in a data warehouse. The risk profile changed; the tooling didn't.
