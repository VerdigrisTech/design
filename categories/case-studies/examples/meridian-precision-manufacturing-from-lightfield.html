Executive summary

A precision manufacturer with three production sites had been running uncoordinated energy-cost reviews per factory, each on a different cadence and using different tooling. Verdigris instrumented Phase 1 at the lead site, then replicated the same scope to a second factory, then to a third, and finally layered a quarterly analytics review on top of all three. 01Problem

Three factories, three monitoring stacks, one finance team that couldn't compare them.

The customer operates three precision-manufacturing factories on different continents. Each site had grown its own facilities-monitoring stack over a decade, optimized for the building-management questions of its era: HVAC scheduling, peak-demand alarms, on-site solar reconciliation. None of the three was instrumented to answer the question that had become the most operationally relevant: which production lines drive the energy bill, and at what tempo do they drive it?

The finance team's framing: "Every quarter we get three energy reports, in three formats, with three different definitions of 'baseline.' The factory managers each defend their numbers, and we can't compare them."

The operations team had attempted side-by-side analytics before, twice. Both attempts stalled at the integration boundary — the second factory's BMS exported different units than the first, and the third factory's logs were sampled at a cadence that defeated waveform-level analysis. The diagnostic question wasn't being answered because the diagnostic data wasn't comparable.

02Approach

Phase by phase, factory by factory, with the third one running its own expansion case.

Verdigris ran a deliberate phased rollout. Phase 1 instrumented the highest-density factory's critical lines on Verdigris meters streaming continuous 8 kHz waveform data into the Signals platform. Three discipline choices defined the engagement:

  • Identical scope, identical tooling, identical reporting cadence per factory. The customer's prior attempts failed because each site negotiated a custom scope. Verdigris held the scope constant so the cross-site comparison would be valid by construction.
  • Lead site first, expansion site second, scale site third. Phase 1 at the most-instrumentable factory built operator confidence and gave finance the first apples-to-apples baseline. Phase 2 replicated the playbook at the expansion site, with the lead site's operators co-presenting the rollout. Phase 3 closed the loop at the third factory, where the customer's own analytics team did the comparative reporting.
  • Quarterly analytics layered on top, not bolted on. Once all three sites streamed comparable data, the customer added a quarterly analytics package as a recurring engagement. Verdigris did not sell the analytics in Phase 1; it earned it by Phase 3.

The full engagement spanned eight quarters across the three sites, with Phase 1 closing in early 2024 and Phase 3 going live in mid-2026. The repeating quarterly cadence is now the customer's default reporting model for any new factory addition.
