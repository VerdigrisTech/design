01Problem

Calendar refreshes, not condition refreshes.

A Fortune 50 telecom was refreshing its UPS rectifier fleet on a fixed five-year cadence across four metro regions. The cadence was set conservatively because the only operational signal available was post-failure: rectifiers entered alarm only after a fault had already propagated, sometimes after a service interruption. The team's options were to keep replacing on schedule (and over-replace healthy equipment) or to push past the schedule (and risk the 4% of units that were degrading silently below standard alarm thresholds).

The reliability lead's framing: "We're spending CapEx like an insurance policy because we can't see the actuarial table."

The team had tried sensor-based monitoring before. Each prior attempt failed at the resolution boundary — sampling at 1 Hz or below averaged away the harmonic structure that carries the precursor signal. The math was unforgiving: a 200 ms inrush event produces 1,600 sample points at 8 kHz and 0.2 sample points at 1 Hz. The diagnostic question wasn't being answered because the diagnostic data didn't exist.

02Approach

8 kHz waveform telemetry on every monitored circuit, continuous.

Verdigris instrumented the 800-rectifier fleet with high-resolution meters streaming continuous 8 kHz waveform data into the Signals platform. Three discipline choices defined the engagement:

  • No selective monitoring. Every rectifier, every region, continuously. Sampling subsets defeats precursor detection because the failures don't pre-announce themselves spatially.
  • Harmonic structure as the signal, not power factor as the proxy. The 7th harmonic specifically tracks electrolytic-capacitor ESR; watching it directly is a cleaner signal than watching aggregated power-quality indices.
  • Operator workflow integration. Findings landed in the existing reliability ticketing system, not a Verdigris-side dashboard. The operations team didn't need to learn a new tool to act on the new visibility.

The pilot ran for 18 months across all four regions before any outcome metric was finalized. The first nine months built the baseline; the second nine months found the first failures the system caught before standard alarms fired.

03Outcome

Capital deferred. Outages avoided. Refresh policy rewritten.

Anchor outcome
$1.3M-$3M

Three-year fleet projection of deferred CapEx-style replacements, achieved by extending the useful life of equipment whose health was previously assumed-failing rather than measured. Range reflects sensitivity to refresh-cadence policy choices the customer's finance team is still tuning.

The single anchor was the deferral. Surrounding observations supporting the anchor:

  • 4% of monitored rectifiers showed degradation below standard alarm thresholds — invisible to the previous monitoring stack.
  • 14-day median advance warning on the precursors that did surface, sufficient for planned-maintenance scheduling instead of unplanned outage absorption.
  • Refresh-cadence policy rewritten from a five-year fixed schedule to a condition-based schedule with a five-year ceiling. The ceiling preserves the conservative posture; the condition trigger captures the savings.
