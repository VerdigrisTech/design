Voice Specimen

Seven Ingredients. One Voice.

The Verdigris voice system is an EQ board, not a preset. Every piece of content mixes the same seven ingredients at different levels. This page renders the full system: ingredients, team profiles, recipes, and emotional outcomes.

The EQ Board

Voice Ingredients

Each ingredient is a quality of writing that can be dialed up or down. Content mixes multiple ingredients. No content uses just one. Think of these like EQ bands, not presets.

Technical Precision
Specificity with units, numbers, and named systems. Physics over adjectives. Describes what things DO, not what they ARE.
Sounds like
"continuous waveforms at 8 kHz"
"A single rack of high-density GPU compute draws over 120 kW of pulsating, non-linear load"
"Design margins have compressed from 30% to 10-15%"
Does not sound like
"advanced monitoring capabilities"
"cutting-edge technology"
"industry-leading precision"
Dial guidance
Up for: operators, engineers, technical evaluators
Down for: executives skimming for strategic value
Primary sources
Jon (founder), Josh (field devices), Mike (competitor analysis)
Strategic Narrative
Why this matters beyond the technical. Where the industry is going and where Verdigris fits. Thesis-complication-resolution structure.
Sounds like
"The entire AI buildout is being erected on a grid that was not designed for it"
"That thesis turned out to be only half right"
"Energy and compute are no longer separate system problems. They are the same problem, expressed in the same signal."
Does not sound like
"We are disrupting the energy monitoring space"
"Positioned to capitalize on the growing AI infrastructure market"
Dial guidance
Up for: executives, investors, strategic partners, press
Down for: practitioners who want specs not stories
Primary sources
Mark (founder), Jimit (outside-in market framing)
Operator Empathy
Writing that signals "we've been in your control room." Uses operator language, acknowledges real constraints, describes problems the way operators experience them.
Sounds like
"the stuff that surprises operators during high-density buildouts"
"the monitoring systems built for the last generation poll at one-second intervals. GPU workloads ramp in eight milliseconds."
Does not sound like
"optimize your facility operations"
"gain unprecedented visibility"
"empower your team with actionable insights"
Dial guidance
Up for: data center ops, facilities engineers, SRE
Down for: investors, candidates
Primary sources
Jon + Thomas (founders), Josh (field work), Seren (customer experience), Mike (operator buying process)
Self-Honesty
Admitting what we got wrong, where we are, and what we don't know yet. The ingredient that makes everything else credible. Without it, precision sounds like marketing and narrative sounds like a pitch.
Sounds like
"That thesis turned out to be only half right"
"Twenty people. Lean by design."
"They are not broken. They are mismatched."
Does not sound like
"we are the market leader"
"a fast-growing team of talented individuals"
Dial guidance
Up for: everything -- should always be present at some level
Down for: never fully off, even data sheets benefit from honest caveats
Primary sources
Mark (publicly self-critical), Jon (technically honest), Jimit, Seren, Mike
Mission Gravity
The weight of what we're working on. Not bragging about impact but letting the problem speak for itself. The grid, the AI buildout, the scale. Inspires without performing inspiration.
Sounds like
"sustain and enrich human life through responsive energy intelligence"
"the most consequential problem in AI infrastructure"
"We are building toward that future, one circuit at a time."
Does not sound like
"we're changing the world"
"revolutionizing the industry"
"on a mission to transform..."
Dial guidance
Up for: careers, about page, fundraising, press
Down for: technical docs, data sheets, support materials
Primary sources
Mark (founder), Jimit (connects work to market significance)
Warmth & Humor
Making people feel good. Levity in technical content. Human moments in corporate communications. Humor that comes from specificity, not from trying to be funny.
Sounds like
"We've been hammering on this thing since 2017 (Fly.io, but this energy)"
"this is the part where we realized we'd been measuring the wrong thing for three weeks"
Does not sound like
"exciting to announce!!!"
emoji-heavy social posts
forced jokes in product copy
Dial guidance
Up for: blog posts, social media, team comms, career pages
Down for: investor decks, data sheets, compliance docs
Primary sources
Josh (builder energy), Seren (understated dry humor), all three founders
Personal Connection
Named people with real context. Not "our team" but specific names. Not "our advisors" but who, and why they matter. Real people make brands real.
Sounds like
"a $600 utility bill arrived at Mark's house"
"Chiqun Zhang, Verdigris's former head of data science, was working on GPUs at Microsoft"
"They know our capabilities because they've watched us build"
Does not sound like
"our world-class team"
"industry-leading advisors"
stock-photo-level anonymity
Dial guidance
Up for: about page, case studies, blog posts, recruiting
Down for: product pages, data sheets
Primary sources
Mark (stories about people), Thomas (highlights team), Seren (empathetic assessment), Jimit (names specifics)

Same Story, Four Voices

Side-by-Side Comparison

One topic -- "UPS rectifier degradation detected at T-Mobile" -- written in four different voice mixes. Same facts, different ingredients, different feelings produced.

Mike Mahedy
Field Report Register
The T-Mobile site in Dallas flagged a UPS rectifier showing elevated ripple current -- 14% above baseline, trending upward over 72 hours. Checked the Starline tap-off data for comparison. Their system showed the unit within tolerance. Ours caught the drift because we're sampling at 8 kHz; their polling interval would have missed the transient spikes entirely. Ops team confirmed: the rectifier was pulling unevenly across phases. They scheduled replacement before it cascaded. Not sure how widespread this pattern is across other T-Mobile sites, honestly. Worth checking.
Jon Chu
Technical Precision Register
The rectifier degradation presented as a progressive increase in output ripple -- from 2.1% to 3.8% THD over 21 days, with characteristic third-harmonic content at 150 Hz indicating asymmetric conduction in the bridge. At 8 kHz sampling, we captured the sub-cycle transient signature: a 4 ms spike pattern repeating every 16.7 ms, consistent with a single diode softening. Traditional 1-second polling sees this as noise. Continuous waveform capture sees it as a countdown.
Mark Chung
Strategic Narrative Register
Here is the thing about UPS failures: they don't announce themselves. The T-Mobile site was running fine by every metric their existing monitoring could produce. But the electrical signal was telling a different story -- one that only shows up when you're listening at 8,000 samples per second instead of one. We caught a rectifier 21 days before it would have failed. That's not a product feature. That's the thesis: the infrastructure supporting AI was built in an era when you could afford to wait for things to break. You can't anymore.
Jimit Shah
Market Advisor Register
This is the pattern that separates monitoring from intelligence. Schneider's and Eaton's UPS platforms report status -- green, yellow, red. They told T-Mobile everything was green. Verdigris told them a rectifier was 21 days from failure. That's the difference between a dashboard and a decision layer. The industry is moving from polling to continuous capture, but nobody in the L2 monitoring layer has the physics engine to interpret what continuous data actually means. That's the wedge.

Emotional Outcomes

Feelings Compass

These are real feelings, not cognitive assessments. "They should understand our value prop" is not a feeling. Every piece of Verdigris content should produce at least one of these. The best content produces 2-3 simultaneously.

Relief
"Finally, someone who gets it." The reader has been dealing with a problem nobody else seems to understand the way they experience it. Verdigris content should make them exhale.
Produced by: operator empathy (naming their problems accurately), technical precision (showing we measure what others can't), personal connection (we've been in their world for 15 years)
Aim for: product pages, case studies, partner materials -- anywhere the reader has a problem they need solved
Curiosity
"I want to know more about how this works." Not marketing-driven "learn more" but genuine intellectual pull. The reader encounters something they haven't thought about before.
Produced by: technical precision (a specific number that surprises), strategic narrative (a framing that recontextualizes), self-honesty (an admission that makes them lean in)
Aim for: technical blogs, homepage, LinkedIn -- anywhere you want the reader to go deeper
Confidence
"These people know what they're doing." Not confidence in the product -- confidence in the PEOPLE. The reader trusts the team because the writing demonstrates depth, honesty, and earned authority.
Produced by: technical precision (depth signals competence), self-honesty (admitting errors signals integrity), personal connection (real people with real histories)
Aim for: homepage, investor updates, partner materials -- anywhere trust is the conversion
Warmth
"I like these people." Not "I respect their product" but genuine human warmth. The feeling that turns evaluators into advocates within their organization.
Produced by: warmth and humor (human moments, specific celebrations), personal connection (named people, real stories), operator empathy (understanding creates closeness)
Aim for: about page, case studies, LinkedIn, career pages -- anywhere you want the reader to become an advocate
Belonging
"I want to be part of this." The reader sees themselves in the mission, the team, or the problem being solved. The feeling that converts candidates, closes partnerships, turns customers into references.
Produced by: mission gravity (the problem is worth their career), warmth and humor (they'd enjoy working here), self-honesty (the company is real, not performing)
Aim for: careers pages, about page, recruiting outreach -- anywhere you want the reader to join
Respect
"These people take the work seriously." Different from confidence (which is about competence). Respect is about craft, standards, and caring about getting it right -- even in how they write.
Produced by: technical precision (specificity signals care), self-honesty (accountability signals standards), mission gravity (taking the problem seriously)
Aim for: technical blogs, investor updates, product pages -- anywhere the reader evaluates quality
