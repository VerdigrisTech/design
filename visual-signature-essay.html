The question

What visual element is uniquely ours?

Every breakout B2B brand derives its visual motif from the product. Stripe's gradients represent money flowing. Cloudflare's entropy wall IS their security. We capture electrical behavior at 8,000 samples per second. That data, made visible, is a design asset no competitor can replicate.

The design model

Quiet Reader +
Precision Instrument

We studied ten contemporary design models. The ones that persist are built on functional traditions: book design (500 years), instrumentation (150 years), mathematical grids (80 years). The ones that date are built on aesthetic trends: scroll animations, gradient meshes, dark-mode-everything.

From the Quiet Reader: a narrow column of precise writing, with full-bleed visualizations breaking the column as standalone moments. Typography whispers. Sections are tall because of breathing room, not content volume.

From the Precision Instrument: data visualizations carry the domain credibility. Monospace for measurements. Functional color. The Lissajous, the spectrogram, and the harmonic spectrum are treated like Craig Mod treats photography -- they expand, stand alone, and say "look how beautiful this data is."

Killed directions

What we won't be doing

Generic sine waves
Without harmonic structure, they could belong to any company.
Particle systems not derived from electrical data
Decorative, not meaningful. The data must be the source.
"What is your building hiding?"
Condescending to our audience. Facility managers know their buildings.
Sales-forward tiles ($2.4M, rotating stats)
Billboard in a work meeting. The adversarial review was right to kill these.
Three-phase chromatic offset on the Lissajous
Technically dishonest. Real three-phase V-I doesn't produce rotated copies of the same figure.
Full palette sweep conflicting with Turn accent rules
The effect must terminate at the section's designated accent color, not cycle freely.
